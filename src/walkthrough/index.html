Unconfirmed DOI

Journal & Article Pages 6 of 7

Meta Report Panel

Click the FLoRA tab to open the Meta Report panel. It lists every known replication and reproduction study color-coded by outcome, flags retractions and expressions of concern, and shows which references have their own entry in the Atlas or are discussed on PubPeer. If the paper is itself a replication, it shows the original study instead.

Outcome badges
Each replication entry shows "success", "failed", or "mixed" — color-coded for quick scanning.
Retraction & concern flags
A red "Retracted" tag or an amber "Concern" tag appears beside the title and any affected reference. Click to read the notice.
"View in Atlas" tag
Shown next to a reference that has its own entry in the FORRT Replication Atlas. Click it to open that reference's report.
PubPeer comments
References discussed on PubPeer show a teal comment pill with the count. The article's thread loads inline — if it can't be embedded, a link to open it on PubPeer is shown.

Journal & Article Pages 7 of 7

Open Access & Retractions

As FLoRA scans a page it flags retracted papers and surfaces free full text. A small toast shows while it works, and it only runs on the tab you're actually viewing.

Open Access padlock
The padlock segment lights up when a free full text exists (via Unpaywall) and stays dimmed when none was found. Open the pill to follow the link.
Retraction flags
The pill's last segment turns into a red ! for a retraction, or an amber one for an expression of concern, replacing the replication count. Open the pill to read the notice.
Scanning indicator
A small toast in the corner appears while FLoRA is scanning the page, then disappears when it's done.
Active tab only
FLoRA only works on the tab you're viewing — background tabs stay idle until you switch to them.
