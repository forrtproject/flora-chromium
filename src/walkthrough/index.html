Meta Report Panel

Click the FLoRA tab to open the Meta Report panel. It lists every known replication and reproduction study color-coded by outcome, flags retractions and expressions of concern, links to free full text where available, and shows references with PubPeer comments. If the paper is itself a replication, it shows the original study instead.

Outcome badges
Each replication entry shows "success", "failed", or "mixed" — color-coded for quick scanning.
Retraction & concern flags
A red "Retracted" or "Concern" tag appears beside the title and any affected reference. Click to read the notice.
Open Access links
When a free full text exists (via Unpaywall), a green "Free" link is added to the DOI — one click to the open copy.
PubPeer comments
References discussed on PubPeer show a teal comment pill with the count. The article's thread loads inline — if it can't be embedded, a link to open it on PubPeer is shown.

Open Access & Retractions

As FLoRA scans a page it flags retracted papers and surfaces free full text. A small toast shows while it works, and it only runs on the tab you're actually viewing.

"Free" Open Access link
Reference DOIs with a free full text (via Unpaywall) get a green "Free" link inside the pill — one click to the open copy.
Retraction flags
Retracted papers and expressions of concern are flagged with a red tag beside the title and DOIs. Click to read the notice.
Scanning indicator
A small toast in the corner appears while FLoRA is scanning the page, then disappears when it's done.
Active tab only
FLoRA only works on the tab you're viewing — background tabs stay idle until you switch to them.
